    Documents and Legal Support

    In order to ensure the right to stay in the country as a migrant or refugee, it is necessary to follow some rules to register. Below, we present guidance for obtaining essential documents and legal information for regularizing the stay of migrants and refugees in Brazil.

    Refugee Request Protocol

    The protocol is a document authorizing temporary residence, valid while the refugee request is being judged by CONARE. The analysis process can take 2 to 3 years, and the protocol is valid for the practice of civil acts, such as opening a bank account or enrolling in courses.

    The protocol must be renewed annually while the refugee process is not completed. Make sure to keep your contact information always updated in CONARE, through the email cadastro.conare@mj.gov.br.

    Naturalization

    Naturalization is the process of acquiring Brazilian nationality, and there are various ways to obtain it. Proof of knowledge in Portuguese language is required, and detailed information about the naturalization process and the required documents can be found on the gov.br website. Click here.

    CPF Registration - Individual

    The Cadastro de Pessoas Físicas- CPF (Individual Taxpayer Registry) is the inclusion of a person as a taxpayer in the Federal Revenue Service of Brazil's register, and it is mandatory for Brazilian or foreign individuals who own assets and rights subject to public registration in Brazil.

    Any individual, regardless of age and whether or not they reside in Brazilian territory, can apply for a CPF registration. A CPF is required to carry out practically any contract, purchase or service, especially financial operations, such as opening bank accounts and applying for credit cards.

    For a foreigner who is not a resident of Brazil to apply for their CPF, they must contact the Brazilian Embassy or Consulate in their country of origin. It is no longer necessary to physically go to the Embassy or Consulate. The foreigner must present an official identification document with a photo that proves their nationality and date of birth. In the case of a minor, they must also present the identification document of the responsible person.
