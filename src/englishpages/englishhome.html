    Detachs

    Mental Healthy

    Immigrating involves experiencing stressful situations that pose a risk to mental health in relation to two factors: the vulnerability of the immigrant and the obstacles encountered in adapting to the host country. Find tips for staying mentally healthy and learn how to obtain free professional help.

    Social Assistance and Women

    In Brazil, Social Assistance is a right for all people, regardless of their nationality or migration status. Through it, it is possible to enroll in various social programs, report violations of rights, and find various support services for women in situations of violence and vulnerability.

    Job Market

    The job market in Brazil can be challenging for immigrants and refugees, especially for those who don't speak Portuguese fluently. However, there are opportunities in sectors such as technology, healthcare, and education. It's also important to be aware of the country's labor laws and regulations and to seek help from organizations that assist immigrants in the job market. Perseverance and adaptability are essential to overcoming obstacles and succeeding in the job search in Brazil.

    Social Integration

    Brazil is known for its cultural and ethnic diversity, and the integration of immigrants is an important topic in Brazilian society. The country has programs to help immigrants adapt and integrate into the job market, such as issuing documents and Portuguese language courses. The social integration of immigrants in Brazil is a two-way street, with benefits for both immigrants and Brazilian society as a whole.
